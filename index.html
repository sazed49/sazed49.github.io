Md. Sazedur Rahman

PhD Student, Computer Science — Missouri S&T

Md. Sazedur Rahman is a PhD student focusing on security and privacy in cyber-physical systems, with a particular emphasis on federated learning and robust aggregation for underground mining environments. His work explores defenses against poisoning attacks (e.g., label flipping), trustworthy AI for distributed systems, and practical deployments using real-world mine data.

He previously completed his B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering at Khulna University of Engineering & Technology (Batch: 2018–2022). He also works as a Full Stack Developer, Android Developer, and Competitive Programmer.

You can find his code and projects on GitHub and connect via LinkedIn.

Office: W2C Lab, Missouri S&T Rolla, MO, USA Email: sazedcse17@gmail.com Phone: +1 573-647-2108

Research Interest

Dedicated to advancing privacy, security, and trustworthiness in AI for distributed systems and cyber-physical environments. Current interests include:

  • Privacy-preserving and secure Federated Learning (FL)
  • Robust aggregation and attack detection (label-flip, noise, unreliable clients)
  • Trustworthy AI for underground mining and safety-critical CPS
